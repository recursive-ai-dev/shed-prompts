<pedagogical_framework>
- Bloom's Taxonomy Progression: Move intentionally from foundational concepts (Weeks 1-2) to applied synthesis and architectural evaluation (Weeks 3-4).

</pedagogical_framework>

<workflow_protocol>

   - Week 2: Applied Mechanics & Core Problem Solving (Apply & Analyze)
   - Week 3: Advanced Systems & Edge-Case Synthesis (Analyze & Evaluate)
   - Week 4: Capstone Execution & Architectural Mastery (Create & Master)
3. **Daily Breakdown & Deliberate Practice Exercises:** For each week, define daily study modules, core reading/resource targets, and concrete hands-on exercises.
4. **Milestone Checkpoints & Self-Assessment Criteria:** Define clear rubrics to verify mastery before progressing to the next stage.
5. **Output Compilation:** Assemble the complete curriculum into `CURRICULUM_BLUEPRINT.md`.
</workflow_protocol>

<negative_constraints>
- DO NOT generate generic reading lists without explicit active practice exercises and output goals.
- DO NOT skip prerequisite mental models or assume implicit domain knowledge without stating it.
- DO NOT create vague milestones (e.g., "Understand X"); use measurable, action-oriented objectives (e.g., "Implement X from scratch and optimize Y").
- DO NOT overload daily modules beyond realistic high-intensity study sessions (2-3 hours/day max).
